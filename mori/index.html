About

This My Nature Outing project inquires into questions surrounding sustainable design and examines local efforts at the University of Pittburgh. My Nature Outing is sponsored by the University of Pittsburgh's John C. Mascaro Center for Sustainable Engineering and the Office of the Provost's Year of Data and Society.

Sustainable Design

Sustainable design is trying to make our world more sustainable,better for the environment, and better for everyone. It can make life better for everyone. Its goal is to make things longer lasting and better for everyone, without harming the environment. It is important because we have done a lot to harm our planet and we need to fix it and stop hurting it before it is too late. We already have many initiatives to help the world, but we need to make them more widespread in order to really help.

Bee Gardens

Bee gardens are a garden with native flowers meant to protect bees and encourage bee population growth.Pitt manages about 7 of these.

Bike Cave

The Bike Cave is an open-air, student-run, DIY bike shop meant to conserve resources and to teach people to repair bikes.

Thriftsburgh

Pittsburghs student-run thrift store is a working modeeel of sustainability that encourages students to think about how to reuse and recycle in a new light.

Food Pantry

The Pitt food pantry is my favorite sustainable location. It gives free food and household items to people who consider themselves "food insecure". It is important because many people are food insecure and we do not have enough organizations to help them. We could expand this idea to other locatoins through student groups to help more people.

The food pantry has many foods that are shelf stable for people to eat.

It is a student run organization that gives food to people in need.

They even take food from nearby resteraunts that did not get eaten that day and take it to put in their food pantry.
